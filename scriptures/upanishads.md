## Chapter 1: Isha Upanishad


### Verse 1
**Sanskrit:** ॐ ईशा वास्यमिदम् सर्वं यत्किञ्च जगत्यां जगत्। तेन त्यक्तेन भुञ्जीथा मा गृधः कस्यस्विद्धनम् ॥
**English:** Om. All this - whatsoever moves on the earth - should be covered by the Lord (Ish). Protect (your Self) through that detachment. Do not covet anybody's wealth. (Or - Do not covet, for whose is wealth?)

### Verse 2
**Sanskrit:** कुर्वन्नेवेह कर्माणि जिजीविषेच्छतम् समाः। एवं त्वयि नान्यथेतोऽस्ति न कर्म लिप्यते नरे ॥
**English:** By doing karma, indeed, should one wish to live here for a hundred years. For a man, such as you (who wants to live thus), there is no way other than this, whereby karma may not cling to you.

### Verse 3
**Sanskrit:** असुर्या नाम ते लोका अन्धेन तमसाऽऽवृताः। ताम्स्ते प्रेत्याभिगच्छन्ति ये के चात्महनो जनाः
**English:** Those worlds of devils are covered by blinding darkness. Those people that kill the Self go to them after giving up this body.

### Verse 4
**Sanskrit:** अनेजदेकं मनसो जवीयो नैनद्देवा आप्नुवन्पूर्वमर्षत्। तद्धावतोऽन्यानत्येति तिष्ठत्तस्मिन्नपो मातरिश्वा दधाति
**English:** It is unmoving, one, and faster than the mind. The senses could not overtake It, since It had run ahead. Remaining stationary, It outruns all other runners. It being there, Matarisva (मातरिश्वा) allots (or supports) all activities.

### Verse 5
**Sanskrit:** तदेजति तन्नेजति तद्दूरे तद्वन्तिके। तदन्तरस्य सर्वस्य तदु सर्वस्यास्य बाह्यतः
**English:** That moves, That does not move; That is far off, That is very near; That is inside all this, and That is also outside all this.

### Verse 6
**Sanskrit:** यस्तु सर्वाणि भूतान्यात्मन्येवानुपश्यति। सर्वभूतेषु चात्मानं ततो न विजुगुप्सते
**English:** He who sees all beings in the Self itself, and the Self in all beings, feels no hatred by virtue of that (realization).

### Verse 7
**Sanskrit:** यस्मिन्सर्वाणि भूतान्यात्मैवाभूद्विजानतः। तत्र को मोहः कः शोक एकत्वमनुपश्यतः ॥
**English:** When to the man of realization all beings become the very Self, then what delusion and what sorrow can there be for that seer of oneness? (Or - In the Self, of the man of realization, in which all beings become the Self, what delusion and what sorrow can remain for that seer of oneness?)

### Verse 8
**Sanskrit:** स पर्यगाच्छुक्रमकायमव्रणमस्नाविरम् शुद्धमपापविद्धम्। कविर्मनीषी परिभूः स्वयम्भूर्याथातथ्यतोऽर्थान् व्यदधाच्छाश्वतीभ्यः समाभ्यः ॥
**English:** He is all-pervasive, pure, bodiless, without wound, without sinews, taintless, untouched by sin, omniscient, ruler of the mind, transcendent, and self-existent; he has duly alloted the (respective) duties to the eternal years (i.e. to the eternal creators called by that name).

### Verse 9
**Sanskrit:** अन्धं तमः प्रविशन्ति येऽविद्यामुपासते। ततो भूय इव ते तमो य उ विद्यायाम् रताः ॥
**English:** Those who worship avidya (rites) enter into blinding darkness; but into greater darkness than that enter they who are engaged in vidya (meditation).

### Verse 10
**Sanskrit:** अन्यदेवाहुर्विद्ययाऽन्यदाहुरविद्यया। इति शुश्रुम धीराणां ये नस्तद्विचचक्षिरे ॥
**English:** They say that by vidya a really different result (is achieved), and they say that by avidya a different result (is achieved)', thus have we heard (the teaching) of those wise men who explained that to us.

### Verse 11
**Sanskrit:** विद्यां चाविद्यां च यस्तद्वेदोभयम् सह। अविद्यया मृत्युं तीर्त्वा विद्ययाऽमृतमश्नुते ॥
**English:** He who knows these two, vidya and avidya, together, attains immortality through vidya, by crossing over death through avidya.

### Verse 12
**Sanskrit:** अन्धं तमः प्रविशन्ति येऽसम्भूतिमुपासते। ततो भूय इव ते तमो य उ सम्भूत्याम् रताः
**English:** Those who worship the Unmanifested (Prakrti) enter into blinding darkness; but those who are devoted to the Manifested (Hiranyagarbha) enter into greater darkness.

### Verse 13
**Sanskrit:** अन्यदेवाहुः सम्भवादन्यदाहुरसम्भवात्। इति शुश्रुम धीराणां ये नस्तद्विचचक्षिरे ॥
**English:** They spoke of a different result indeed from the worship of the Manifested, and they spoke of a different result from the worship of the Unmanifested' - thus we have heard (the teaching) of those wise men who explained that to us.

### Verse 14
**Sanskrit:** सम्भूतिं च विनाशं च यस्तद्वेदोभयम् सह। विनाशेन मृत्युं तीर्त्वा सम्भूत्याऽमृतमश्नुते ।।
**English:** He who knows these two, the Unmanifested and the Destruction (Hiranyagarbha), together, attains immortality through the Unmanifested by crossing death through Destruction.

### Verse 15
**Sanskrit:** हिरण्मयेन पात्रेण सत्यस्यापिहितं मुखम्। तत्त्वं पूषन्नपावृणु सत्यधर्माय दृष्टये ॥
**English:** The face of truth (Brahman in the solar orb) is concealed by a golden vessel. Do thou, O Sun, open it so as to be seen by me who am by nature truthful (or, am the performer of rightful duties).

### Verse 16
**Sanskrit:** पूषन्नेकर्षे यम सूर्य प्राजापत्य व्यूह रश्मीन् समूह तेजः। यत्ते रूपं कल्याणतमं तत्ते पश्यामि योऽसावसौ पुरुषः सोऽहमस्मि ॥
**English:** O thou who art nourisher, the solitary traveller, the controller, the acquirer, the son of Prajapati, do remove thy rays, do gather up thy dazzle. I shall behold by thy grace that form of thine which is most benign. I am that very Person that is yonder (in the Sun).

### Verse 17
**Sanskrit:** वायुरनिलममृतमथेदं भस्मांतम् शरीरम्। ॐ क्रतो स्मर कृतम् स्मर क्रतो स्मर कृतम् स्मर
**English:** Let (my) vital force now attain the (all pervading) immortal Air; (and) now let this body be reduced to ashes. Om, O mind, remember - remember all that has been done. O mind, remember - remember all that has been done.

### Verse 18
**Sanskrit:** अग्ने नय सुपथा राये अस्मान् विश्वानि देव वयुनानि विद्वान्। युयोध्यस्मज्जुहुराणमेनो भूयिष्ठां ते नमउक्तिं विधेम॥
**English:** O Fire! O God! Knowing, as thou do, all our deeds, lead us by the good path for the enjoyment of the fruits of our deeds; remove from us all crooked sins. We offer thee many words of salutation.

## Chapter 2: Katha Upanishad — Chapter 1.1


### Verse 1
**Sanskrit:** ॐ उशन् ह वै वाजश्रवसः सर्ववेदसं ददौ । तस्य ह नचिकेता नाम पुत्र आस ॥
**English:** Yearning ( superior rewards,) Vajasravasa gifted all his material wealth. He had a son named Nachiketa.

### Verse 2
**Sanskrit:** तँ ह कुमारँ सन्तं दक्षिणासु नीयमानासु श्रद्धाविवेश सोऽमन्यत ॥
**English:** On the gifts being fetched for giving , Nachiketa, though a young lad, was overcome with religious fervour and he pondered :

### Verse 3
**Sanskrit:** पीतोदका जग्धतृणा दुग्धदोहा निरिन्द्रियाः । अनन्दा नाम ते लोकास्तान् स गच्छति ता ददत् ॥
**English:** Joyless, indeed, are the worlds to which he goes who gives as gifts such old cows that cannot calve nor produce milk any more. They had also drank water for the last time and eaten grass terminally. (Giving away useless articles as gift in a sacrifice is a sin and cannot bring good reward.)

### Verse 4
**Sanskrit:** स होवाच पितरं तत कस्मै मां दास्यसीति । द्वितीयं तृतीयं तँ होवाच मृत्यवे त्वा ददामीति ॥
**English:** He addressed his father : "Father, to whom will you give me away?" He repeatedly said so, twice and thrice. Upon this the father replied : "I shall give you to Death"

### Verse 5
**Sanskrit:** बहूनामेमि प्रथमो बहूनामेमि मध्यमः । किँ स्विद्यमस्य कर्तव्यं यन्मयाऽद्य करिष्यति ॥
**English:** (Nachiketa thought within himself) : Among many of (my father's disciples), I am either at the top or middle (but never at the bottom ). What then will he achieve in giving me to Yama ?

### Verse 6
**Sanskrit:** अनुपश्य यथा पूर्वे प्रतिपश्य तथाऽपरे । सस्यमिव मर्त्यः पच्यते सस्यमिवाजायते पुनः ॥
**English:** Remember how your forefathers behaved . Consider how men of worth conduct themselves today. Like vegetation man ripens and decays, and like vegetation he is born again .

### Verse 7
**Sanskrit:** वैश्वानरः प्रविशत्यतिथिर्ब्राह्मणो गृहान् । तस्यैताँ शान्तिं कुर्वन्ति हर वैवस्वतोदकम् ॥
**English:** (Nachiketa goes to the abode of Yama, but the latter was not at home. Nachiketa had to wait for three days without food. On Yama's return, he is told by his people): A Brahmana guest entering a house-hold resembles fire. He is offered appeasement. O ! Vaivasvata (Yama), fetch water (for washing the feet and sipping).

### Verse 8
**Sanskrit:** आशाप्रतीक्षे संगतँ सूनृतां चेष्टापूर्ते पुत्रपशूँश्च सर्वान् । एतद्वृङ्क्ते पुरुषस्याल्पमेधसो यस्यानश्नन्वसति ब्राह्मणो गृहे ॥
**English:** Hopes and expectations, the benefits of association with the good, the merit arising from sweet and beneficial speech, the deeds prescribed in the scriptures and moral codes , children and cattle, all these are destroyed in the case of the person of dull intellect in whose house a holy guest has remained unattended.

### Verse 9
**Sanskrit:** तिस्रो रात्रीर्यदवात्सीर्गृहे मे- ऽनश्नन् ब्रह्मन्नतिथिर्नमस्यः । नमस्तेऽस्तु ब्रह्मन् स्वस्ति मेऽस्तु तस्मात्प्रति त्रीन्वरान्वृणीष्व ॥
**English:** Yama said to Nachiketa : O holy and venerable guest, since you have dwelt in my house for three nights without food, choose three boons from me. I bow to you; may all be well with me.

### Verse 10
**Sanskrit:** शान्तसंकल्पः सुमना यथा स्याद् वीतमन्युर्गौतमो माऽभि मृत्यो । त्वत्प्रसृष्टं माऽभिवदेत्प्रतीत एतत् त्रयाणां प्रथमं वरं वृणे ॥
**English:** (Nachiketa thereupon said) - O Death, may I have the first of the three boons to (my father) Gautama being in happy frame of mind and of peaceful disposition anger; and may he recognise me and receive me when I am released by you.

### Verse 11
**Sanskrit:** यथा पुरस्ताद् भविता प्रतीत औद्दालकिरारुणिर्मत्प्रसृष्टः । सुखँ रात्रीः शयिता वीतमन्यु स्त्वां ददृशिवान्मृत्युमुखात् प्रमुक्तम् ॥
**English:** (Yama said :) By my order, Auddalaki Aruni (your father) will sleep peacefully at night. He will recognise you and treat you as in the past, having seen you released from the jaws of death. And he will be without anger . (The mental agony caused to Nachiketa's father has been removed by the first boon).

### Verse 12
**Sanskrit:** स्वर्गे लोके न भयं किंचनास्ति न तत्र त्वं न जरया बिभेति । उभे तीर्त्वाऽशनायापिपासे शोकातिगो मोदते स्वर्गलोके ॥
**English:** (Nachiketa said:) In heaven there is no fear at all ; death and old age are not there. Having overcome hunger and thirst and all sorrow, one rejoices in the heavenly world.

### Verse 13
**Sanskrit:** स त्वमग्निँ स्वर्ग्यमध्येषि मृत्यो प्रब्रूहि त्वँ श्रद्दधानाय मह्यम् । स्वर्गलोका अमृतत्वं भजन्त एतद् द्वितीयेन वृणे वरेण ॥
**English:** O Death, You know the Fire (sacrifice) that leads to heaven. Instruct me on that who am full of faith . Those in heaven are deathless. This I choose as my second boon.

### Verse 14
**Sanskrit:** प्र ते ब्रवीमि तदु मे निबोध स्वर्ग्यमग्निं नचिकेतः प्रजानन् । अनन्तलोकाप्तिमथो प्रतिष्ठां विद्धि त्वमेतं निहितं गुहायाम् ॥
**English:** O Nachiketa, I know about the Fire that leads to heaven. This I shall impart to you . Listen to me carefully. Know this to be the means of attaining eternal heaven and also the support of the world. This knowledge is hidden in the cave of the heart.

### Verse 15
**Sanskrit:** लोकादिमग्निं तमुवाच तस्मै या इष्टका यावतीर्वा यथा वा । स चापि तत्प्रत्यवदद्यथोक्त मथास्य मृत्युः पुनरेवाह तुष्टः ॥
**English:** Yama then explained that Fire (sacrifice), is the origin of the world. He gave details of what type of bricks and how many are required (for the altar) and how the fire is to be lit. And Nachiketa repeated all the instructions given to him. Then Yama, being pleased at this, said again.

### Verse 16
**Sanskrit:** तमब्रवीत् प्रीयमाणो महात्मा वरं तवेहाद्य ददामि भूयः । तवैव नाम्ना भविताऽयमग्निः सृङ्कां चेमामनेकरूपां गृहाण ॥
**English:** That high-souled (Yama) happily, said to Nachiketa "I shall here and now give you another boon: may this fire be named after you; and may you also accept this multi-coloured garland.

### Verse 17
**Sanskrit:** त्रिणाचिकेतस्त्रिभिरेत्य सन्धिं त्रिकर्मकृत्तरति जन्ममृत्यू । ब्रह्मजज्ञं देवमीड्यं विदित्वा निचाय्येमाँ शान्तिमत्यन्तमेति ॥
**English:** He transcends cyclic existence who has perfomed the Nachiketa fire-sacrifice three times and has received instructions from the three (mother,father and preceptor), and has also discharged his three-fold duties (study of Vedas, performance of Sacrifice and giving alms), having realised the all pervading shining one worthy of worship-the fire god born of Brahman. Such a person also attains supreme peace.

### Verse 18
**Sanskrit:** त्रिणाचिकेतस्त्रयमेतद्विदित्वा य एवं विद्वाँश्चिनुते नाचिकेतम् । स मृत्युपाशान् पुरतः प्रणोद्य शोकातिगो मोदते स्वर्गलोके ॥
**English:** The man of wisdom, having known the injunctions performs the Nachiketa sacrifice three times. Thus annhilating the noose of death even before the fall of his body, crosses the (ocean of ) grief and enjoys in heaven.

### Verse 19
**Sanskrit:** एष तेऽग्निर्नचिकेतः स्वर्ग्यो यमवृणीथा द्वितीयेन वरेण । एतमग्निं तवैव प्रवक्ष्यन्ति जनासः तृतीयं वरं नचिकेतो वृणीष्व ॥
**English:** This is your fire, which takes one to heaven and which you have chosen as your second boon. People will designate this fire after your name. Now choose your third boon, O Nachiketa.

### Verse 20
**Sanskrit:** येयं प्रेते विचिकित्सा मनुष्ये- ऽस्तीत्येके नायमस्तीति चैके । एतद्विद्यामनुशिष्टस्त्वयाऽहं वराणामेष वरस्तृतीयः ॥
**English:** (Nachiketa said): "Upon the death of a person, there arises this doubt - some declare that something outlives the body, other say, nothing remains. I desire to know, upon your instructions, about this issue and this is my third among the boons"

### Verse 21
**Sanskrit:** देवैरत्रापि विचिकित्सितं पुरा न हि सुविज्ञेयमणुरेष धर्मः । अन्यं वरं नचिकेतो वृणीष्व मा मोपरोत्सीरति मा सृजैनम् ॥
**English:** (Yama said) : " This subtle matter is not easily understood. In the days of yore, even Gods had entairtained doubts about this. Therefore, O! Nachiketa! choose another boon. Do not press this and release me from this (Obligation)"

### Verse 22
**Sanskrit:** देवैरत्रापि विचिकित्सितं किल त्वं च मृत्यो यन्न सुज्ञेयमात्थ । वक्ता चास्य त्वादृगन्यो न लभ्यो नान्यो वरस्तुल्य एतस्य कश्चित् ॥
**English:** (Nachiketa said) : " O! Death! you too say that even the gods had indeed doubted about this and is not also easy to comprehend. There is neither a teacher equal to you nor a boon equal to this".

### Verse 23
**Sanskrit:** शतायुषः पुत्रपौत्रान्वृणीष्वा बहून्पशून् हस्तिहिरण्यमश्वान् । भूमेर्महदायतनं वृणीष्व स्वयं च जीव शरदो यावदिच्छसि ॥
**English:** (Yama said) : "May you seek sons and grandsons who will live a hundred years, cattle, elephants, horses and gold, as also an extensive land area to rule and live any number of autumns as you like.

### Verse 24
**Sanskrit:** एतत्तुल्यं यदि मन्यसे वरं वृणीष्व वित्तं चिरजीविकां च । महाभूमौ नचिकेतस्त्वमेधि कामानां त्वा कामभाजं करोमि ॥
**English:** If you think so, seek another boon equal to this for riches, longivity and an expansive kingdom where O! Nachiketa ! May you rule over. I shall make you the enjoyer of all your desires.

### Verse 25
**Sanskrit:** ये ये कामा दुर्लभा मर्त्यलोके सर्वान् कामाँश्छन्दतः प्रार्थयस्व । इमा रामाः सरथाः सतूर्या न हीदृशा लम्भनीया मनुष्यैः । आभिर्मत्प्रत्ताभिः परिचारयस्व नचिकेतो मरणं माऽनुप्राक्षीः ॥
**English:** Whatever desires are there which are not easy to be gained in this world of mortals, all those may you obtain as you desire. These beautiful damsels along with chariots and musical instruments, which are not attainable by men, I shall bestow upon you. Entertain yourself with these. Do not seek to know about death.

### Verse 26
**Sanskrit:** श्वोभावा मर्त्यस्य यदन्तकैतत् सर्वेंद्रियाणां जरयन्ति तेजः । अपि सर्वं जीवितमल्पमेव तवैव वाहास्तव नृत्यगीते ॥
**English:** "O! Death! All these are impermanent. The sense organs of man is worn out through indulgence in these enjoyments. The entire life is but brief. You may keep these vehicles, music and dance to yourself.

### Verse 27
**Sanskrit:** न वित्तेन तर्पणीयो मनुष्यो लप्स्यामहे वित्तमद्राक्ष्म चेत्त्वा । जीविष्यामो यावदीशिष्यसि त्वं वरस्तु मे वरणीयः स एव ॥
**English:** No man is ever satisfied with wealth. Having seen you, we shall live as long as you rule. The boon I desire is , therefore , the one I have sought.

### Verse 28
**Sanskrit:** अजीर्यताममृतानामुपेत्य जीर्यन्मर्त्यः क्वधःस्थः प्रजानन् । अभिध्यायन् वर्णरतिप्रमोदान् अतिदीर्घे जीविते को रमेत ॥
**English:** Once the immortal and everlasting one is attained, which man, who is himself nor permanent, will be elated or be thought of living long after examining the worthless enjoyments of song & dance ?

### Verse 29
**Sanskrit:** यस्मिन्निदं विचिकित्सन्ति मृत्यो यत्साम्पराये महति ब्रूहि नस्तत् । योऽयं वरो गूढमनुप्रविष्टो नान्यं तस्मान्नचिकेता वृणीते ॥
**English:** O! Death! Instruct me on the superior life after death regarding which people have reservations. Nachiketa chooses not other boon other than this mysterious one.

## Chapter 3: Katha Upanishad — Chapter 1.2


### Verse 1
**Sanskrit:** अन्यच्छ्रेयोऽन्यदुतैव प्रेय- स्ते उभे नानार्थे पुरुषँ सिनीतः । तयोः श्रेय आददानस्य साधु भवति हीयतेऽर्थाद्य उ प्रेयो वृणीते ॥
**English:** Two concepts bind the man and are of different objectives. One is what is beneficial and the other is what is pleasing. He who chooses the beneficial, gains and the one who goes for the the pleasant, deflects from that.

### Verse 2
**Sanskrit:** श्रेयश्च प्रेयश्च मनुष्यमेतः तौ सम्परीत्य विविनक्ति धीरः । श्रेयो हि धीरोऽभि प्रेयसो वृणीते प्रेयो मन्दो योगक्षेमाद्वृणीते ॥
**English:** Both these - beneficial and pleasant - come to humans. The discriminating one after due consideration selects the beneficial alone in preference to the pleasure-giving; but desirous of worldly happiness, the dull-witted falls for pleasing.

### Verse 3
**Sanskrit:** स त्वं प्रियान्प्रियरूपांश्च कामान् अभिध्यायन्नचिकेतोऽत्यस्राक्षीः । नैतां सृङ्कां वित्तमयीमवाप्तो यस्यां मज्जन्ति बहवो मनुष्याः ॥
**English:** O! Nachiketa!, After due consideration, you have rejected all those pleasures and objects of pleasures, which also tempt. You have not got into this path of pleasures, in which many men are lost.

### Verse 4
**Sanskrit:** दूरमेते विपरीते विषूची अविद्या या च विद्येति ज्ञाता । विद्याभीप्सिनं नचिकेतसं मन्ये न त्वा कामा बहवोऽलोलुपन्त ॥
**English:** Widely varying with different destinations are these two- ignorance and knowledge. I think Nachiketas is desirous of knowledge as the objects of pleasure do not tempt him.

### Verse 5
**Sanskrit:** अविद्यायामन्तरे वर्तमानाः स्वयं धीराः पण्डितंमन्यमानाः । दन्द्रम्यमाणाः परियन्ति मूढा अन्धेनैव नीयमाना यथान्धाः ॥
**English:** The deluded staying in illusion thinking themselves to be enlightened go in circles faltering and floundering like sightless-men led by a sightless one.

### Verse 6
**Sanskrit:** न साम्परायः प्रतिभाति बालं प्रमाद्यन्तं वित्तमोहेन मूढम् । अयं लोको नास्ति पर इति मानी पुनः पुनर्वशमापद्यते मे ॥
**English:** Deluded by riches, one in illusion like a child thinks only of this world and not of a hereafter - and thus, gets under my control repeatedly.

### Verse 7
**Sanskrit:** श्रवणायापि बहुभिर्यो न लभ्यः श‍ृण्वन्तोऽपि बहवो यं न विद्युः । आश्चर्यो वक्ता कुशलोऽस्य लब्धा आश्चर्यो ज्ञाता कुशलानुशिष्टः ॥
**English:** It is not possible for many to hear about it (Brahman) and even having heard many do not understand. The teacher must be a skilful one and the pupil should be sharp in grasp. The knower is a wonderful person instructed by the adept.

### Verse 8
**Sanskrit:** न नरेणावरेण प्रोक्त एष सुविज्ञेयो बहुधा चिन्त्यमानः । अनन्यप्रोक्ते गतिरत्र नास्ति अणीयान् ह्यतर्क्यमणुप्रमाणात् ॥
**English:** This principle (Brahman) is not understood by pondering in different ways or being taught by one of low ability. There is no other way than being taught by one of unparalleled ability and it transcends all arguments and is subtlest of subtle.

### Verse 9
**Sanskrit:** नैषा तर्केण मतिरापनेया प्रोक्तान्येनैव सुज्ञानाय प्रेष्ठ । यां त्वमापः सत्यधृतिर्बतासि त्वादृङ्नो भूयान्नचिकेतः प्रष्टा ॥
**English:** This knowledge is not attainable by argumentation. It is of easy comprehension only when instructed by another. O! Dearest one! O! Nachiketa! let there be seekers like you! You are truly resolved in your desire.

### Verse 10
**Sanskrit:** जानाम्यहं शेवधिरित्यनित्यं न ह्यध्रुवैः प्राप्यते हि ध्रुवं तत् । ततो मया नाचिकेतश्चितोऽग्निः अनित्यैर्द्रव्यैः प्राप्तवानस्मि नित्यम् ॥
**English:** I am aware that riches are not permanent. Certainly what is everlasting is not attained through what is temporary. But, I have gained the eternal by performing the nachiketa fire-sacrifice with transient materials.

### Verse 11
**Sanskrit:** कामस्याप्तिं जगतः प्रतिष्ठां क्रतोरानन्त्यमभयस्य पारम् । स्तोममहदुरुगायं प्रतिष्ठां दृष्ट्वा धृत्या धीरो नचिकेतोऽत्यस्राक्षीः ॥
**English:** O! Nachiketa! Having seen that great and exalted state, the very foundation of the universe, the end-point of all observances, the other shore of fear, You wise one ! You have rejected the attainment of all desires, being steadfast in you resolve.

### Verse 12
**Sanskrit:** तं दुर्दर्शं गूढमनुप्रविष्टं गुहाहितं गह्वरेष्ठं पुराणम् । अध्यात्मयोगाधिगमेन देवं मत्वा धीरो हर्षशोकौ जहाति ॥
**English:** The wise man having, by meditating on it , got that which is difficult to realise, effulgent, deep-seated and immanent, dwelling in the cave of the heart transcends both joy and sorrow.

### Verse 13
**Sanskrit:** एतच्छ्रुत्वा सम्परिगृह्य मर्त्यः प्रवृह्य धर्म्यमणुमेतमाप्य । स मोदते मोदनीयँ हि लब्ध्वा विवृतँ सद्म नचिकेतसं मन्ये ॥
**English:** The mortal, having heard and realised that subtle principle, the essence of righteousness, rejoices verily, in a blissful state, having attained the bliss-worthy one. I feel that for Nachiketa , its residence is dis-covered.

### Verse 14
**Sanskrit:** अन्यत्र धर्मादन्यत्राधर्मा- दन्यत्रास्मात्कृताकृतात् । अन्यत्र भूताच्च भव्याच्च यत्तत्पश्यसि तद्वद ॥
**English:** (Nachiketas said): Please tell me that which you perceive as variant from the right and the wrong, the cause and effect and the past and the future.

### Verse 15
**Sanskrit:** सर्वे वेदा यत्पदमामनन्ति तपाꣳसि सर्वाणि च यद्वदन्ति । यदिच्छन्तो ब्रह्मचर्यं चरन्ति तत्ते पदꣳ सङ्ग्रहेण ब्रवीम्योमित्येतत् ॥
**English:** That which is proclaimed by all the scriptures, which is said to be the end effect of all rituals and for which enquiry into the ultimate reality is undertaken, I shall tell you about it briefly as "OM"

### Verse 16
**Sanskrit:** एतद्ध्येवाक्षरं ब्रह्म एतद्ध्येवाक्षरं परम् । एतद्ध्येवाक्षरं ज्ञात्वा यो यदिच्छति तस्य तत् ॥
**English:** This syllable (OM) is the highest (Brahman) , this alone is ever-lasting and whoever realises this attains whatever he desires.

### Verse 17
**Sanskrit:** एतदालम्बनँ श्रेष्ठमेतदालम्बनं परम् । एतदालम्बनं ज्ञात्वा ब्रह्मलोके महीयते ॥
**English:** This (syllable OM) is the best support, this is the supreme support and upon realising this, one is adored in the world of Brahma (Hiranyagarbha loka).

### Verse 18
**Sanskrit:** न जायते म्रियते वा विपश्चि- न्नायं कुतश्चिन्न बभूव कश्चित् । अजो नित्यः शाश्वतोऽयं पुराणो न हन्यते हन्यमाने शरीरे ॥
**English:** The consciousness (soul ) is neither born nor is it subject to death. It has not manifested from anything nor has it given rise to a new entity. This one is unborn, always existing, everlasting, indeterminable in antiquity and is not destroyed when the body (container) is destroyed.

### Verse 19
**Sanskrit:** हन्ता चेन्मन्यते हन्तुँ हतश्चेन्मन्यते हतम् । उभौ तौ न विजानीतो नायँ हन्ति न हन्यते ॥
**English:** If the slayer thinks he slays, and slain thinks that he is slain, neither of them knows the truth. This (Knower) neither slays nor is slain.

### Verse 20
**Sanskrit:** अणोरणीयान्महतो महीया- नात्माऽस्य जन्तोर्निहितो गुहायाम् । तमक्रतुः पश्यति वीतशोको धातुप्रसादान्महिमानमात्मनः ॥
**English:** Smaller than the smallest, greater than the greatest is the Atman that dwells in the cave of the heart of beings. He who is desireless, realises the glory of Atman through cleansing of mind and senses, and transcends from sorrow.

### Verse 21
**Sanskrit:** आसीनो दूरं व्रजति शयानो याति सर्वतः । कस्तं मदामदं देवं मदन्यो ज्ञातुमर्हति ॥
**English:** While seated, he travels far ; lying down he goes all-over. Who can know other than me that Shining One, who is both joyful and joyless.

### Verse 22
**Sanskrit:** अशरीरँ शरीरेष्वनवस्थेष्ववस्थितम् । महान्तं विभुमात्मानं मत्वा धीरो न शोचति ॥
**English:** Having realised the all-pervading Supreme Atman, who is bodiless within the bodies, stable amidst the unstable, the wise one grieves no more.

### Verse 23
**Sanskrit:** नायमात्मा प्रवचनेन लभ्यो न मेधया न बहुना श्रुतेन । यमेवैष वृणुते तेन लभ्यः तस्यैष आत्मा विवृणुते तनूꣳ स्वाम् ॥
**English:** By the study of the scriptures, lecturing one's own intellectual enquiries or by wide learning or listening, this Atman cannot be realised. Whomsoever, It chooses alone, can attain that realisation about It, as It reveals Its own nature to him.

### Verse 24
**Sanskrit:** नाविरतो दुश्चरितान्नाशान्तो नासमाहितः । नाशान्तमानसो वाऽपि प्रज्ञानेनैनमाप्नुयात् ॥
**English:** He who is not free from evil conduct, whose senses are not controlled , whose emotions are not harmonised, whose mind is not at peace, cannot attain this Atman by any means of knowledge.

### Verse 25
**Sanskrit:** यस्य ब्रह्म च क्षत्रं च उभे भवत ओदनः । मृत्युर्यस्योपसेचनं क इत्था वेद यत्र सः ॥
**English:** He for whom the Brahmana and the Kshatriya are food, and Death the pickle, who can know where He (The Self, Atman) is ?

## Chapter 4: Katha Upanishad — Chapter 1.3


### Verse 1
**Sanskrit:** ऋतं पिबन्तौ सुकृतस्य लोके गुहां प्रविष्टौ परमे परार्धे । छायातपौ ब्रह्मविदो वदन्ति पञ्चाग्नयो ये च त्रिणाचिकेताः ॥
**English:** The cave of the heart is occupied by two entities (the cosmic self and the individual self), enjoying the fruits of their actions. Those who are realised, call them light and shade, as also the tenders of the five fires (house-holders), who had performed Nachiketa sacrifice thrice.

### Verse 2
**Sanskrit:** यः सेतुरीजानानामक्षरं ब्रह्म यत् परम् । अभयं तितीर्षतां पारं नाचिकेतँ शकेमहि ॥
**English:** We can perform the Nachiketa sacrifice which will take us to the celestial regions, acting as a bridge. we could also attain the knowledge about the everlasting principle, Supreme Brahman, which is the place beyond the realm of fear and the refuge of the seekers of liberation - from human bondage.

### Verse 3
**Sanskrit:** आत्मानँ रथितं विद्धि शरीरँ रथमेव तु । बुद्धिं तु सारथिं विद्धि मनः प्रग्रहमेव च ॥
**English:** The inner being is the controller (seated in) of the chariot, which is the body, the intellect the driver and the mind the reins.

### Verse 4
**Sanskrit:** इन्द्रियाणि हयानाहुर्विषयाँ स्तेषु गोचरान् । आत्मेन्द्रियमनोयुक्तं भोक्तेत्याहुर्मनीषिणः ॥
**English:** They (the wise) say that the sense organs are the horses, the road is the sense objects and the experiencer is the body, sense organs and the mind, say the realised ones.

### Verse 5
**Sanskrit:** यस्त्वविज्ञानवान्भवत्ययुक्तेन मनसा सदा । तस्येन्द्रियाण्यवश्यानि दुष्टाश्वा इव सारथेः ॥
**English:** That One whose understanding of the reality is not there and whose mind is without restraint, his sense organs behave without control like the rogue horses of a charioteer.

### Verse 6
**Sanskrit:** यस्तु विज्ञानवान्भवति युक्तेन मनसा सदा । तस्येन्द्रियाणि वश्यानि सदश्वा इव सारथेः ॥
**English:** But he who has right understanding and whose mind is always restrained, his senses become disciplined like the obedient horses of a charioteer.

### Verse 7
**Sanskrit:** यस्त्वविज्ञानवान्भवत्यमनस्कः सदाऽशुचिः । न स तत्पदमाप्नोति संसारं चाधिगच्छति ॥
**English:** Transmigratory (cyclic) existence is for him who has no control over his mind, lacks the right understanding and is always contaminated in mind, as he never attains that goal.

### Verse 8
**Sanskrit:** यस्तु विज्ञानवान्भवति समनस्कः सदा शुचिः । स तु तत्पदमाप्नोति यस्माद्भूयो न जायते ॥
**English:** However, that one who is discriminating , has his mind under restraint and always pure (in thought), verily he attains that destination, from where he never reverts to re-birth.

### Verse 9
**Sanskrit:** विज्ञानसारथिर्यस्तु मनः प्रग्रहवान्नरः । सोऽध्वनः पारमाप्नोति तद्विष्णोः परमं पदम् ॥
**English:** That person who has his (discriminative) intelligence as his charioteer and whose mind has been reined in , reaches the terminus of the journey, which is that supreme abode of Lord Vishnu.

### Verse 10
**Sanskrit:** इन्द्रियेभ्यः परा ह्यर्था अर्थेभ्यश्च परं मनः । मनसस्तु परा बुद्धिर्बुद्धेरात्मा महान्परः ॥
**English:** The objects of senses are superior to the sense organs, the mind is superior to the sense objects, the intellect is greater than the mind and the great Atman is superior to the intellect .

### Verse 11
**Sanskrit:** महतः परमव्यक्तमव्यक्तात्पुरुषः परः । पुरुषान्न परं किंचित्सा काष्ठा सा परा गतिः ॥
**English:** The Unmanifested is higher than the great ( Atman), and the Purusha higher than the Unmanifested. That is nothing superior to the Purusha, it is the acme and the supreme refuge.

### Verse 12
**Sanskrit:** एष सर्वेषु भूतेषु गूढोऽऽत्मा न प्रकाशते । दृश्यते त्वग्र्यया बुद्ध्या सूक्ष्मया सूक्ष्मदर्शिभिः ॥
**English:** This Atman, is not seen, though laid secretly in all beings. However, it is seen by the Seers of the subtle , through their incisive and subtle power of their intellect.

### Verse 13
**Sanskrit:** यच्छेद्वाङ्मनसी प्राज्ञस्तद्यच्छेज्ज्ञान आत्मनि । ज्ञानमात्मनि महति नियच्छेत्तद्यच्छेच्छान्त आत्मनि ॥
**English:** The wise should dissolve the speech in the mind, the mind in the intellect, the intellect in the Great Atman , and that Great Atman in the Atman of quietitude.

### Verse 14
**Sanskrit:** उत्तिष्ठत जाग्रत प्राप्य वरान्निबोधत । क्षुरस्य धारा निशिता दुरत्यया दुर्गं पथस्तत्कवयो वदन्ति ॥
**English:** Arise ! Awake! Having resorted to an excellent preceptor , realise ( the Reality ) . The people with far-sight say that like the sharp edge of a razor, that path is both difficult to set foot upon and to cross.

### Verse 15
**Sanskrit:** अशब्दमस्पर्शमरूपमव्ययं तथाऽरसं नित्यमगन्धवच्च यत् । अनाद्यनन्तं महतः परं ध्रुवं निचाय्य तन्मृत्युमुखात् प्रमुच्यते ॥
**English:** One gets rid of the cyclic existence of births and deaths on realising that ultimate Reality, which is devoid of sound, touch, form, perishability, taste, smell and which is constant, beyond the Mahat, does not undergo any change and has neither a beginning nor an end.

### Verse 16
**Sanskrit:** नाचिकेतमुपाख्यानं मृत्युप्रोक्तँ सनातनम् । उक्त्वा श्रुत्वा च मेधावी ब्रह्मलोके महीयते ॥
**English:** Having listened attentively to the ancient episode of Nachiketa, as narrated and discussed by the god of Death , the Wise One revels in the world of Brahma and is glorified.

### Verse 17
**Sanskrit:** य इमं परमं गुह्यं श्रावयेद् ब्रह्मसंसदि । प्रयतः श्राद्धकाले वा तदानन्त्याय कल्पते । तदानन्त्याय कल्पत इति ॥
**English:** Whoever relates with devotion , this highly secret-laden episode in the assembly of the learned or at the time of performing rites and rituals for the Infinitude , indeed becomes fit for the Infinitude.

## Chapter 5: Katha Upanishad — Chapter 2.1


### Verse 1
**Sanskrit:** पराञ्चि खानि व्यतृणत् स्वयम्भू- स्तस्मात्पराङ्पश्यति नान्तरात्मन् । कश्चिद्धीरः प्रत्यगात्मानमैक्ष- दावृत्तचक्षुरमृतत्वमिच्छन् ॥
**English:** The Self-Created (God) made the senses turn outward; hence one looks outward, does not see what is within oneself. Rare is the wise man, who, longing for immortality, turns his eyes inward and sees the inner Atman.

### Verse 2
**Sanskrit:** पराचः कामाननुयन्ति बाला- स्ते मृत्योर्यन्ति विततस्य पाशम् । अथ धीरा अमृतत्वं विदित्वा ध्रुवमध्रुवेष्विह न प्रार्थयन्ते ॥
**English:** The ignorant pursue outward pleasures and walk into the wide-spread net of death. The wise, however, recognising the eternal , among the things that are fleeting , do not seek anything .

### Verse 3
**Sanskrit:** येन रूपं रसं गन्धं शब्दान् स्पर्शाꣳश्च मैथुनान् । एतेनैव विजानाति किमत्र परिशिष्यते । एतद्वै तत् ॥
**English:** That by which man sees, tastes, smells, hears and enjoys, is the omniscient Lord. By that alone man has knowledge. What is there that remains unknown to It? This is indeed That (Self that Nachiketa was asking about).

### Verse 4
**Sanskrit:** स्वप्नान्तं जागरितान्तं चोभौ येनानुपश्यति । महान्तं विभुमात्मानं मत्वा धीरो न शोचति ॥
**English:** That by which one perceives things both in dream and in the waking state - having known that as the great omni-present Self, the wise man does not grieve.

### Verse 5
**Sanskrit:** य इमं मध्वदं वेद आत्मानं जीवमन्तिकात् । ईशानं भूतभव्यस्य न ततो विजुगुप्सते । एतद्वै तत् ॥
**English:** He who knows the Atman, the enjoyer of the fruits of action, sustainer of life, as the Lord of the past and future, as close at hand -- he fears no more. This is indeed That (Self that Nachiketa was asking about).

### Verse 6
**Sanskrit:** यः पूर्वं तपसो जातमद्भ्यः पूर्वमजायत । गुहां प्रविश्य तिष्ठन्तं यो भूतेभिर्व्यपश्यत । एतद्वै तत् ॥
**English:** He who sees the First-Born - born of knowledge, born before the creation of waters - sees Him within the heart, dwelling among the elements , sees Brahman indeed. This is verily That (Self that Nachiketa was asking about).

### Verse 7
**Sanskrit:** या प्राणेन संभवत्यदितिर्देवतामयी । गुहां प्रविश्य तिष्ठन्तीं या भूतेभिर्व्यजायत । एतद्वै तत् ॥
**English:** Aditi, the power of all powers, manifested in the form of Prana, embodied in the elements, who resides in the heart, is the Immortal Self. This is truly That (Self that Nachiketa was asking about).

### Verse 8
**Sanskrit:** अरण्योर्निहितो जातवेदा गर्भ इव सुभृतो गर्भिणीभिः । दिवे दिवे ईड्यो जागृवद्भिर्हविष्मद्भिर्मनुष्येभिरग्निः । एतद्वै तत् ॥
**English:** Like the child well guarded in the womb by the pregnant women, the all-knowing Agni, who lies hidden in the fire-sticks, is worshipped every day by the awakened man and by those who offer oblations in the Fire. This is verily that (Self that Nachiketa was asking about).

### Verse 9
**Sanskrit:** यतश्चोदेति सूर्योऽस्तं यत्र च गच्छति । तं देवाः सर्वेऽर्पितास्तदु नात्येति कश्चन । एतद्वै तत् ॥
**English:** The Atman is really that from which the sun rises and into which it mixes again. To That (Atman) are all the Gods attached and no one can actually go beyond it (Self or Atman that Nachiketa was asking about).

### Verse 10
**Sanskrit:** यदेवेह तदमुत्र यदमुत्र तदन्विह । मृत्योः स मृत्युमाप्नोति य इह नानेव पश्यति ॥
**English:** Whatever is here is also there; whatever is there is the same here. He who sees a difference here travels from death to death.

### Verse 11
**Sanskrit:** मनसैवेदमाप्तव्यं नेह नानाऽस्ति किंचन । मृत्योः स मृत्युं गच्छति य इह नानेव पश्यति ॥
**English:** By the purified mind alone is This to be realised. There is no multiplicity here. From death to death he goes , who sees as if there is difference here.

### Verse 12
**Sanskrit:** अङ्गुष्ठमात्रः पुरुषो मध्य आत्मनि तिष्ठति । ईशानं भूतभव्यस्य न ततो विजुगुप्सते । एतद्वै तत् ॥
**English:** The Purusha of the size of a thumb dwells within the body. He is the lord of the past and future. After knowing Him one fears no more. This is verily That (Self that Nachiketa was asking about).

### Verse 13
**Sanskrit:** अङ्गुष्ठमात्रः पुरुषो ज्योतिरिवाधूमकः । ईशानो भूतभव्यस्य स एवाद्य स उ श्वः । एतद्वै तत् ॥
**English:** The thumbsized Purusha , the lord of the past and the future, is like a flame without smoke; He stays the same today and tomorrow. This is verily That. (Self that Nachiketa was asking about).

### Verse 14
**Sanskrit:** यथोदकं दुर्गे वृष्टं पर्वतेषु विधावति । एवं धर्मान् पृथक् पश्यंस्तानेवानुविधावति ॥
**English:** As rain, fallen on a hill, streams down its sides, so runs he after many births, who sees manifoldness in the Self.

### Verse 15
**Sanskrit:** यथोदकं शुद्धे शुद्धमासिक्तं तादृगेव भवति । एवं मुनेर्विजानत आत्मा भवति गौतम ॥
**English:** As water that is pure, when poured into water that is pure remains pure, so does the Self of the realised one remains pure on knowing (uniting with) Brahman , O! Gautama !

## Chapter 6: Katha Upanishad — Chapter 2.2


### Verse 1
**Sanskrit:** पुरमेकादशद्वारमजस्यावक्रचेतसः । अनुष्ठाय न शोचति विमुक्तश्च विमुच्यते । एतद्वै तत् ॥
**English:** There is the city of eleven gates belonging to Him who is unborn and of unchanging consciousness. By meditating upon Him, one grieves no more; and being liberated one becomes free from birth and death. This is indeed That (Self that Nachiketa was asking about).

### Verse 2
**Sanskrit:** हँसः शुचिषद्वसुरन्तरिक्षसद्- होता वेदिषदतिथिर्दुरोणसत् । नृषद्वरसदृतसद्व्योमसद् अब्जा गोजा ऋतजा अद्रिजा ऋतं बृहत् ॥
**English:** The Atman is the Sun in the heavens, the air in the sky, the fire in the altar, the guest in the house; He dwells in men, in Gods, in the Sacrifice, in space. He is all that is born in water, all that is born on earth, in the sacrifice and on the moutains. He is the True and the Great.

### Verse 3
**Sanskrit:** ऊर्ध्वं प्राणमुन्नयत्यपानं प्रत्यगस्यति । मध्ये वामनमासीनं विश्वे देवा उपासते ॥
**English:** He it is that leads the life-breath upwards and casts the lower breath downwards. All the senses minister to Him, the Adorable, who is seated in the centre.

### Verse 4
**Sanskrit:** अस्य विस्रंसमानस्य शरीरस्थस्य देहिनः । देहाद्विमुच्यमानस्य किमत्र परिशिष्यते । एतद्वै तत् ॥
**English:** When the soul in the body is separated and leaves the body what is left over here? This is indeed That. (Self that Nachiketa was asking about).

### Verse 5
**Sanskrit:** न प्राणेन नापानेन मर्त्यो जीवति कश्चन । इतरेण तु जीवन्ति यस्मिन्नेतावुपाश्रितौ ॥
**English:** Man does not live by breath alone, but by Him who is the power of breath.

### Verse 6
**Sanskrit:** हन्त त इदं प्रवक्ष्यामि गुह्यं ब्रह्म सनातनम् । यथा च मरणं प्राप्य आत्मा भवति गौतम ॥
**English:** Listen, I shall tell you O ! Gautama, of the secret, eternal Brahman, and what happens to the self after meeting death.

### Verse 7
**Sanskrit:** योनिमन्ये प्रपद्यन्ते शरीरत्वाय देहिनः । स्थाणुमन्येऽनुसंयन्ति यथाकर्म यथाश्रुतम् ॥
**English:** Some souls seek a womb for the sake of a physical body and yet others go to immovables, like the plant kingdom, depending upon the activities in the past and also in consonance with the level of awakening they have reached.

### Verse 8
**Sanskrit:** य एष सुप्तेषु जागर्ति कामं कामं पुरुषो निर्मिमाणः । तदेव शुक्रं तद्ब्रह्म तदेवामृतमुच्यते । तस्मिँल्लोकाः श्रिताः सर्वे तदु नात्येति कश्चन । एतद्वै तत् ॥
**English:** This principle Purusha keeping awake when others sleep, creating objects of desires , that which is taintless and said to be deathless is the Brahman. That is the substratum of all the worlds, and no one can surpass it . This , indeed is that. (Self that Nachiketa was asking about).

### Verse 9
**Sanskrit:** अग्निर्यथैको भुवनं प्रविष्टो रूपं रूपं प्रतिरूपो बभूव । एकस्तथा सर्वभूतान्तरात्मा रूपं रूपं प्रतिरूपो बहिश्च ॥
**English:** As fire, though one only , having entered the world, takes the form of various objects (which it burns ) so also though the all permeating brahman is one only, takes the form apparently of the various objects it enters and is also beyond them.

### Verse 10
**Sanskrit:** वायुर्यथैको भुवनं प्रविष्टो रूपं रूपं प्रतिरूपो बभूव । एकस्तथा सर्वभूतान्तरात्मा रूपं रूपं प्रतिरूपो बहिश्च ॥
**English:** As one air, takes the shape of every object it enters, so the Self which permeates into everything , though one, takes the shape of every object in which it dwells. It also exists outside them.

### Verse 11
**Sanskrit:** सूर्यो यथा सर्वलोकस्य चक्षुः न लिप्यते चाक्षुषैर्बाह्यदोषैः । एकस्तथा सर्वभूतान्तरात्मा न लिप्यते लोकदुःखेन बाह्यः ॥
**English:** Like the sun, which is the eye of the whole world is not tainted by the outward defects of the objects it lights, which are seen by the eye, in the same manner, this Brahman which is inherent in all beings is not tainted by the sufferings of the world, as it is beyond the world.

### Verse 12
**Sanskrit:** एको वशी सर्वभूतान्तरात्मा एकं रूपं बहुधा यः करोति । तमात्मस्थं येऽनुपश्यन्ति धीराः तेषां सुखं शाश्वतं नेतरेषाम् ॥
**English:** Eternal bliss falls to the lot of those wise ones who realise Him, existing within, the one controller, the inner being of all beings and being are manifests in myriad forms and not to the lot of others.

### Verse 13
**Sanskrit:** नित्योऽनित्यानां चेतनश्चेतनानाम् एको बहूनां यो विदधाति कामान् । तमात्मस्थं येऽनुपश्यन्ति धीराः तेषां शान्तिः शाश्वती नेतरेषाम् ॥
**English:** Those wise persons who realise Him, who is within, who is permanent among all others which are impermanent , who is sentient amongst which are insentinent and though one fulfills the desires of the several, they have lasting (inner) peace and none others.

### Verse 14
**Sanskrit:** तदेतदिति मन्यन्तेऽनिर्देश्यं परमं सुखम् । कथं नु तद्विजानीयां किमु भाति विभाति वा ॥
**English:** How shall I know that supreme, unspeakable Bliss which they realize directly as 'This'? Is it self-effulgent - does It shine dinstinctly, or does It not?

### Verse 15
**Sanskrit:** न तत्र सूर्यो भाति न चन्द्रतारकं नेमा विद्युतो भान्ति कुतोऽयमग्निः । तमेव भान्तमनुभाति सर्वं तस्य भासा सर्वमिदं विभाति ॥
**English:** " Neither the sun, the moon , the stars, nor the lightning shines here, nor to speak of this fire. Every other thing shines following That and due to its effulgence everything is illumined.

## Chapter 7: Katha Upanishad — Chapter 2.3


### Verse 1
**Sanskrit:** ऊर्ध्वमूलोऽवाक्शाख एषोऽश्वत्थः सनातनः । तदेव शुक्रं तद्ब्रह्म तदेवामृतमुच्यते । तस्मिँल्लोकाः श्रिताः सर्वे तदु नात्येति कश्चन । एतद्वै तत् ॥
**English:** With its root above and branches below stands this Banyan-tree. That (root) is indeed the Pure, that is Brahman, the Immortal. All the worlds are contained in It and no one goes beyond It. This is verily That (Self that Nachiketa was asking about).

### Verse 2
**Sanskrit:** यदिदं किं च जगत् सर्वं प्राण एजति निःसृतम् । महद्भयं वज्रमुद्यतं य एतद्विदुरमृतास्ते भवन्ति ॥
**English:** The whole world, whatever there is, springs from and moves in Prana (Brahman), which is a great terror like an upraised thunder-bolt. Those who know This become deathless.

### Verse 3
**Sanskrit:** भयादस्याग्निस्तपति भयात्तपति सूर्यः । भयादिन्द्रश्च वायुश्च मृत्युर्धावति पञ्चमः ॥
**English:** The fire burns, the sun shines and Indra , the Wind God and the God of Death - the fifth in row - perform their tasks, all out of fear for Him.

### Verse 4
**Sanskrit:** इह चेदशकद्बोद्धुं प्राक्षरीरस्य विस्रसः । ततः सर्गेषु लोकेषु शरीरत्वाय कल्पते ॥
**English:** If one is not able to realise ( Him ) before his body falls, he becomes liable to be embodied again in the future creations.

### Verse 5
**Sanskrit:** यथाऽऽदर्शे तथाऽऽत्मनि यथा स्वप्ने तथा पितृलोके । यथाऽप्सु परीव ददृशे तथा गन्धर्वलोके छायातपयोरिव ब्रह्मलोके ॥
**English:** In one's own soul Brahman is realised clearly, as if seen in a mirror. In the world of the ancestors He is perceived as a dream (hazily); in the world of angels as a reflection in water and in the world of Brahma as light distinct from shade.

### Verse 6
**Sanskrit:** इन्द्रियाणां पृथग्भावमुदयास्तमयौ च यत् । पृथगुत्पद्यमानानां मत्वा धीरो न शोचति ॥
**English:** The wise one, having understood the individual nature of the senses, their rising and setting as of things that come into being separate from the soul, does not grieve.

### Verse 7
**Sanskrit:** इन्द्रियेभ्यः परं मनो मनसः सत्त्वमुत्तमम् । सत्त्वादधि महानात्मा महतोऽव्यक्तमुत्तमम् ॥
**English:** Mind is greater than the senses, the discriminating power (Intellect) is Greater than the mind , the Great Brahman is higher than the intellect and the unmanifested is greater than the Atman.

### Verse 8
**Sanskrit:** अव्यक्तात्तु परः पुरुषो व्यापकोऽलिङ्ग एव च । यं ज्ञात्वा मुच्यते जन्तुरमृतत्वं च गच्छति ॥
**English:** The Purusha is beyond the unmanifested , is all-pervading and attributeless , realising That, a creature is liberated and attains to deathlessness.

### Verse 9
**Sanskrit:** न संदृशे तिष्ठति रूपमस्य न चक्षुषा पश्यति कश्चनैनम् । हृदा मनीषा मनसाऽभिक्लृप्तो य एतद्विदुरमृतास्ते भवन्ति ॥
**English:** None beholds Him with the eyes, for He is without visible form. He is revealed in the heart through self-control and meditation. Those who know Him become immortal.

### Verse 10
**Sanskrit:** यदा पञ्चावतिष्ठन्ते ज्ञानानि मनसा सह । बुद्धिश्च न विचेष्टते तामाहुः परमां गतिम् ॥
**English:** When the five senses of perception stand still along with the mind, and when the intellect does not move, that is called the Supreme State.

### Verse 11
**Sanskrit:** तां योगमिति मन्यन्ते स्थिरामिन्द्रियधारणाम् । अप्रमत्तस्तदा भवति योगो हि प्रभवाप्ययौ ॥
**English:** This steadiness of the mind and senses is what is known as Yoga. One becomes watchful at that time, for Yoga is subject to growth and decay.

### Verse 12
**Sanskrit:** नैव वाचा न मनसा प्राप्तुं शक्यो न चक्षुषा । अस्तीति ब्रुवतोऽन्यत्र कथं तदुपलभ्यते ॥
**English:** The Atman cannot be attained by speech, by the mind or by the eye. How can It be realised otherwise than from those who know It as existing?

### Verse 13
**Sanskrit:** अस्तीत्येवोपलब्धव्यस्तत्त्वभावेन चोभयोः । अस्तीत्येवोपलब्धस्य तत्त्वभावः प्रसीदति ॥
**English:** He is to be realised as "existing", and then that It has a transcendental nature. His innermost nature reveals itself to those who apprehend It as existing.

### Verse 14
**Sanskrit:** यदा सर्वे प्रमुच्यन्ते कामा येऽस्य हृदि श्रिताः । अथ मर्त्योऽमृतो भवत्यत्र ब्रह्म समश्नुते ॥
**English:** The mortal gains immortality and in this life becomes Brahman, when all the yearnings in his mind are dissolved.

### Verse 15
**Sanskrit:** यदा सर्वे प्रभिद्यन्ते हृदयस्येह ग्रन्थयः । अथ मर्त्योऽमृतो भवत्येतावद्ध्यनुशासनम् ॥
**English:** In this body, when all those knots in the mind are cut , then the mortal becomes immortal ; thus runs the instruction (in Vedic scriptures).

### Verse 16
**Sanskrit:** शतं चैका च हृदयस्य नाड्य- स्तासां मूर्धानमभिनिःसृतैका । तयोर्ध्वमायन्नमृतत्वमेति विष्वङ्ङन्या उत्क्रमणे भवन्ति ॥
**English:** Of the hundred and one are nerves of the heart; one extends towards the top (crown) of the head. Proceeding upwards through it, man attains deathlessness and the others lead one to different destinations (human or non-human form).

### Verse 17
**Sanskrit:** अङ्गुष्ठमात्रः पुरुषोऽन्तरात्मा सदा जनानां हृदये संनिविष्टः । तं स्वाच्छरीरात्प्रवृहेन्मुञ्जादिवेषीकां धैर्येण । तं विद्याच्छुक्रममृतं तं विद्याच्छुक्रममृतमिति ॥
**English:** In the heart of everybeing dwells, the inner soul, of the dimension of the human thumb , The Purusha, whom through meditation and persverance is like drawing out a thread from the stalk. Him one should know as pure and immortal. Him one should know as pure and immortal.

### Verse 18
**Sanskrit:** मृत्युप्रोक्तां नचिकेतोऽथ लब्ध्वा विद्यामेतां योगविधिं च कृत्स्नम् । ब्रह्मप्राप्तो विरजोऽभूद्विमृत्यु- रन्योऽप्येवं यो विदध्यात्ममेव ॥
**English:** Instructed by Yama , in the knowledge, in this manner, as also in the technique of Yoga Sastra, Nachiketa became sinless and transcended physical death and attained Brahman. Also any other will do so , on realising the Brahman.

## Chapter 8: Mandukya Upanishad


### Verse 1
**Sanskrit:** ॐ इत्येतदक्षरमिदँ सर्वं तस्योपव्याख्यानं भूतं भवद् भविष्यदिति सर्वमोङ्कार एव यच्चान्यत् त्रिकालातीतं तदप्योङ्कार एव
**English:** OM! – This Imperishable Word is the whole of this visible universe. Its explanation is as follows: What has become, what is becoming, what will become, – verily, all of this is OM. And what is beyond these three states of the world of time, – that too, verily, is OM.

### Verse 2
**Sanskrit:** सर्वं ह्येतद् ब्रह्मायमात्मा ब्रह्म सोऽयमात्मा चतुष्पात्
**English:** All this, verily, is Brahman. The Self is Brahman. This Self has four quarters.

### Verse 3
**Sanskrit:** जागरितस्थानो बहिष्प्रज्ञः सप्ताङ्ग एकोनविंशतिमुखः स्थूलभुग्वैश्वानरः प्रथमः पादः
**English:** The first quarter is Vaiśvānara. Its field is the waking state. Its consciousness is outward-turned. It is seven-limbed and nineteen-mouthed. It enjoys gross objects.

### Verse 4
**Sanskrit:** स्वप्नस्थानोऽन्तःप्रज्ञः सप्ताङ्ग एकोनविंशतिमुखः प्रविविक्तभुक्तैजसो द्वितीयः पादः
**English:** The second quarter is taijasa. Its field is the dream state. Its consciousness is inward-turned. It is seven-limbed and nineteen-mouthed. It enjoys subtle objects.

### Verse 5
**Sanskrit:** यत्र सुप्तो न कञ्चन कामं कामयते न कञ्चन स्वप्नं पश्यति तत् सुषुप्तम् । सुषुप्तस्थान एकीभूतः प्रज्ञानघन एवानन्दमयो ह्यानन्दभुक् चेतोमुखः प्राज्ञस्तृतीयः पादः
**English:** The third quarter is prājña, where one asleep neither desires anything nor beholds any dream: that is deep sleep. In this field of dreamless sleep, one becomes undivided, an undifferentiated mass of consciousness, consisting of bliss and feeding on bliss. His mouth is consciousness.

### Verse 6
**Sanskrit:** एष सर्वेश्वरः एष सर्वज्ञ एषोऽन्तर्याम्येष योनिः सर्वस्य प्रभवाप्ययौ हि भूतानाम्
**English:** This is the Lord of All; the Omniscient; the Indwelling Controller; the Source of All. This is the beginning and end of all beings.

### Verse 7
**Sanskrit:** नान्तःप्रज्ञं न बहिष्प्रज्ञं नोभयतःप्रज्ञं न प्रज्ञानघनं न प्रज्ञं नाप्रज्ञम् । अदृष्टमव्यवहार्यमग्राह्यमलक्षणं अचिन्त्यमव्यपदेश्यमेकात्मप्रत्ययसारं प्रपञ्चोपशमं शान्तं शिवमद्वैतं चतुर्थं मन्यन्ते स आत्मा स विज्ञेयः ॥
**English:** That is known as the fourth quarter: neither inward-turned nor outward-turned consciousness, nor the two together; not an indifferentiated mass of consciousness; neither knowing, nor unknowing; invisible, ineffable, intangible, devoid of characteristics, inconceivable, indefinable, its sole essence being the consciousness of its own Self; the coming to rest of all relative existence; utterly quiet; peaceful; blissful: without a second: this is the Ātman, the Self; this is to be realised.

### Verse 8
**Sanskrit:** सोऽयमात्माध्यक्षरमोङ्कारोऽधिमात्रं पादा मात्रा मात्राश्च पादा अकार उकारो मकार इति
**English:** This identical Ātman, or Self, in the realm of sound is the syllable OM, the above described four quarters of the Self being identical with the components of the syllable, and the components of the syllable being identical with the four quarters of the Self. The components of the Syllable are A, U, M.

### Verse 9
**Sanskrit:** जागरितस्थानो वैश्वानरोऽकारः प्रथमा मात्राऽऽप्तेरादिमत्त्वाद् वाऽऽप्नोति ह वै सर्वान् कामानादिश्च भवति य एवं वेद
**English:** Vaiśvānara, whose field is the waking state, is the first sound, A, because this encompasses all, and because it is the first. He who knows thus, encompasses all desirable objects; he becomes the first.

### Verse 10
**Sanskrit:** स्वप्नस्थानस्तैजस उकारो द्वितीया मात्रोत्कर्षात् उभयत्वाद्वोत्कर्षति ह वै ज्ञानसन्ततिं समानश्च भवति नास्याब्रह्मवित्कुले भवति य एवं वेद
**English:** Taijasa, whose field is the dream state, is the second sound, U, because this is an excellence, and contains the qualities of the other two. He who knows thus, exalts the flow of knowledge and becomes equalised; in his family there will be born no one ignorant of Brahman.

### Verse 11
**Sanskrit:** सुषुप्तस्थानः प्राज्ञो मकारस्तृतीया मात्रा मितेरपीतेर्वा मिनोति ह वा इदं सर्वमपीतिश्च भवति य एवं वेद
**English:** Prājña, whose field is deep sleep, is the third sound, M, because this is the measure, and that into which all enters. He who knows thus, measures all and becomes all.

### Verse 12
**Sanskrit:** अमात्रश्चतुर्थोऽव्यवहार्यः प्रपञ्चोपशमः शिवोऽद्वैत एवमोङ्कार आत्मैव संविशत्यात्मनाऽऽत्मानं य एवं वेद
**English:** The fourth is soundless: unutterable, a quieting down of all relative manifestations, blissful, peaceful, non-dual. Thus, OM is the Ātman, verily. He who knows thus, merges his self in the Self; – yea, he who knows thus.
