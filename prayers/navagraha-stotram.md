## Sanskrit

जपाकुसुमसंकाशं काश्यपेयं महाद्युतिम्।
तमोरिं सर्वपापघ्नं प्रणतोऽस्मि दिवाकरम्॥ १॥ (सूर्यः)

दधिशंखतुषाराभं क्षीरोदार्णवसम्भवम्।
नमामि शशिनं सोमं शम्भोर्मुकुटभूषणम्॥ २॥ (चन्द्रः)

धरणीगर्भसम्भूतं विद्युत्कान्तिसमप्रभम्।
कुमारं शक्तिहस्तं तं मंगलं प्रणमाम्यहम्॥ ३॥ (मंगलः)

प्रियंगुकलिकाश्यामं रूपेणाप्रतिमं बुधम्।
सौम्यं सौम्यगुणोपेतं तं बुधं प्रणमाम्यहम्॥ ४॥ (बुधः)

देवानां च ऋषीणां च गुरुं काञ्चनसन्निभम्।
बुद्धिभूतं त्रिलोकेशं तं नमामि बृहस्पतिम्॥ ५॥ (बृहस्पतिः)

हिमकुन्दमृणालाभं दैत्यानां परमं गुरुम्।
सर्वशास्त्रप्रवक्तारं भार्गवं प्रणमाम्यहम्॥ ६॥ (शुक्रः)

नीलांजनसमाभासं रविपुत्रं यमाग्रजम्।
छायामार्तण्डसम्भूतं तं नमामि शनैश्चरम्॥ ७॥ (शनिः)

अर्धकायं महावीर्यं चन्द्रादित्यविमर्दनम्।
सिंहिकागर्भसम्भूतं तं राहुं प्रणमाम्यहम्॥ ८॥ (राहुः)

पलाशपुष्पसंकाशं तारकाग्रहमस्तकम्।
रौद्रं रौद्रात्मकं घोरं तं केतुं प्रणमाम्यहम्॥ ९॥ (केतुः)

इति श्रीव्यासमुखोद्गीतं यः पठेत् सुसमाहितः।
दिवा वा यदि वा रात्रौ विघ्नशान्तिर्भविष्यति॥ १०॥

नरनारीनृपाणां च भवेद्दुःस्वप्ननाशनम्।
ऐश्वर्यमतुलं तेषामारोग्यं पुष्टिवर्धनम्॥ ११॥

ग्रहनक्षत्रजाः पीडास्तस्कराग्निसमुद्भवाः।
ताः सर्वाः प्रशमं यान्ति व्यासो ब्रूते न संशयः॥ १२॥

इति श्रीवेदव्यासविरचितम् आदित्यादिनवग्रहस्तोत्रं सम्पूर्णम्॥

## Transliteration

Japakusuma Sankasham Kashyapeyam Mahadyutim, Tamorim Sarvapapaghnam Pranatosmi Divakaram. (1 - Surya/Sun)

Dadhishankhatusharabham Ksheerodarnavasambhavam, Namami Shashinam Somam Shambhormukutabhushanam. (2 - Chandra/Moon)

Dharanigarbhasambhutam Vidyutkantisamaprabham, Kumaram Shaktihastam Tam Mangalam Pranamamyaham. (3 - Mangala/Mars)

Priyangukalikashyamam Rupenapratimam Budham, Saumyam Saumyagunopetam Tam Budham Pranamamyaham. (4 - Budha/Mercury)

Devanam Cha Rishinam Cha Gurum Kanchanasannibham, Buddhibhutam Trilokesham Tam Namami Brihaspatim. (5 - Brihaspati/Jupiter)

Himakundamrinalabham Daityanam Paramam Gurum, Sarvashastrapravaktaram Bhargavam Pranamamyaham. (6 - Shukra/Venus)

Nilanjanasamabhasam Raviputram Yamagrajam, Chhayamartandasambhutam Tam Namami Shanaishcharam. (7 - Shani/Saturn)

Ardhakayam Mahaviryam Chandradityavimardanam, Simhikagarbhasambhutam Tam Rahum Pranamamyaham. (8 - Rahu)

Palashapushpasankasham Tarakagrahamastakam, Raudram Raudratmakam Ghoram Tam Ketum Pranamamyaham. (9 - Ketu)

Iti Shri Vyasamukhodgitam Yah Pathet Susamahitah, Diva Va Yadi Va Ratrau Vighnashantirbhavishyati. (10)

Naranarinripanam Cha Bhaveddusvapnanashanam, Aishvaryamatulam Tesham Arogyam Pushtivardhanam. (11)

Grahanakshatrajah Pidastaskaragnisamudbhavah, Tah Sarvah Prashamam Yanti Vyaso Brute Na Samshayah. (12)

Iti Shri Vedavyasavirachitam Adityadi Navagraha Stotram Sampurnam.

## Meaning

A hymn of nine verses traditionally attributed to the sage Vyasa, each saluting one of the nine planetary deities (Navagraha) of Vedic astrology: Surya the Sun, bright as a hibiscus flower and son of Kashyapa; Chandra the Moon, white as curds and snow, born of the milky ocean and the crown-jewel of Shiva; Mangala (Mars), born of the Earth, bright as lightning, holding a spear; Budha (Mercury), dark as a privet flower-bud, gentle and full of pleasing qualities; Brihaspati (Jupiter), golden-hued teacher of gods and sages, full of wisdom; Shukra (Venus), white as a lotus stalk, the great preceptor of the demons and teacher of all scriptures; Shani (Saturn), dark as collyrium, son of the Sun, born of Chhaya, elder brother of Yama; Rahu, of half a body and great valour, born from the womb of Simhika, who seizes Sun and Moon; and Ketu, the color of a palasha flower, fierce and terrible, with stars at his head. The closing verses are the Phala Shruti — the promised fruits of reciting this hymn.

## Benefits

Recited to pacify all nine planets simultaneously and harmonize their influence on the devotee's life and birth chart. According to the text itself, one who recites this hymn composed by Vyasa with full concentration, whether by day or night, will have all obstacles removed; men, women and kings alike will be freed from bad dreams and granted unmatched prosperity, good health and nourishment; and all afflictions caused by planets, stars, thieves and fire will be calmed. It is commonly chanted during Navagraha puja, homas (fire rituals), and whenever an astrologer identifies an unfavorable planetary period (dasha) or transit (gochar) in a person's chart.
