## Meaning

A complete garland of 108 sacred names of Goddess Mahalakshmi, each preceded by "Om" and ending in "Namah" (salutations). The names move through her many aspects: as primordial nature and transformation (Prakriti, Vikriti), as knowledge and faith (Vidya, Shraddha), as the lotus-goddess (Padma, Padmalaya, Padmahasta — lotus-eyed, lotus-handed, lotus-born), as the consort of Vishnu (Harivallabha, Vishnupatni, Padmanabhapriya), as the destroyer of poverty and misfortune (Daridryanashini, Daridryadhvamsini), as the moon-faced and four-armed goddess (Chandravadana, Chaturbhuja), and finally as the all-pervading mistress of the fourteen worlds (Bhuvaneshvari) who is one with the Trimurti — Brahma, Vishnu and Shiva.

## Benefits

Reciting all 108 names of Lakshmi is one of the most popular ways to invoke her blessings, especially on Fridays, during Diwali, Kojagara Puja and other Lakshmi-related festivals. Each name is traditionally chanted with an offering of a flower, rice grain, or with mala beads. Regular recitation is believed to attract wealth and prosperity, remove poverty and financial obstacles, bring peace and harmony into the home, and deepen devotion and a sense of divine grace. Many devotees follow it with the Lakshmi Gayatri or Lakshmi Chalisa for a complete worship sequence.
