## Sanskrit

आरती कीजै हनुमान लला की। दुष्ट दलन रघुनाथ कला की॥ जाके बल से गिरिवर काँपे। रोग दोष जाके निकट न झाँके॥ अंजनि पुत्र महा बलदाई। संतन के प्रभु सदा सहाई॥ दे बीरा रघुनाथ पठाए। लंका जारि सिया सुधि लाए॥ लंका सो कोट समुद्र सी खाई। जात पवनसुत बार न लाई॥ लंका जारि असुर संहारे। सियाराम जी के काज सँवारे॥ लक्ष्मण मूर्छित पड़े सकारे। आणि संजीवन प्राण उबारे॥ पैठि पताल तोरि जम कारे। अहिरावण की भुजा उखारे॥ बाएँ भुजा असुर दल मारे। दाहिने भुजा संतजन तारे॥ सुर नर मुनि आरती उतारें। जय जय जय हनुमान उचारें॥ कंचन थार कपूर लौ छाई। आरती करत अंजना माई॥ जो हनुमान जी की आरती गावै। बसहिं बैकुंठ परम पद पावै॥ लंका विध्वंस किये रघुराई। तुलसीदास स्वामी कीर्ति गाई॥

## Meaning

Let us perform the aarti of Hanuman, the child devotee of Raghunath who destroys the wicked — at whose strength mountains tremble and near whom disease and sin dare not approach. Son of Anjani, the great bestower of strength, ever a helper to the saints. Sent by Raghunath, he burned Lanka and brought news of Sita. Lanka with its fort and ocean-like moat — the son of the wind crossed without delay. He burned Lanka, destroyed the demons and fulfilled the mission of Sita and Ram. When Lakshman lay unconscious, he brought the Sanjeevani herb and saved his life. He descended into the netherworld, broke the locks of death and uprooted Ahiravan. With his left arm he destroyed the army of demons, with his right he uplifted the saints. Gods, men and sages perform his aarti, chanting victory, victory, victory to Hanuman. Mother Anjana performs the aarti with a golden plate adorned with the glow of camphor. Whoever sings this aarti of Hanuman shall dwell in Vaikuntha and attain the highest state. Tulsidas sings the glory of the Lord who caused the destruction of Lanka.

## Benefits

The complete devotional aarti of Lord Hanuman as composed by Tulsidas, traditionally sung on Tuesdays and Saturdays. It narrates his key deeds — burning Lanka, saving Lakshman with the Sanjeevani herb, and defeating Ahiravan. The aarti concludes with a promise that sincere singers shall attain Vaikuntha, the abode of the divine.
