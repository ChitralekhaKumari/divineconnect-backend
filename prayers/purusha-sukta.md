## Sanskrit

सहस्रशीर्षा पुरुषः सहस्राक्षः सहस्रपात्।
स भूमिं विश्वतो वृत्वात्यतिष्ठद्दशाङ्गुलम्॥ १॥

पुरुष एवेदं सर्वं यद्भूतं यच्च भव्यम्।
उतामृतत्वस्येशानो यदन्नेनातिरोहति॥ २॥

एतावानस्य महिमातो ज्यायांश्च पूरुषः।
पादोऽस्य विश्वा भूतानि त्रिपादस्यामृतं दिवि॥ ३॥

त्रिपादूर्ध्व उदैत्पूरुषः पादोऽस्येहाभवत्पुनः।
ततो विष्वङ् व्यक्रामत्साशनानशने अभि॥ ४॥

तस्माद्विराळजायत विराजो अधि पूरुषः।
स जातो अत्यरिच्यत पश्चाद्भूमिमथो पुरः॥ ५॥

यत्पुरुषेण हविषा देवा यज्ञमतन्वत।
वसन्तो अस्यासीदाज्यं ग्रीष्म इध्मः शरद्धविः॥ ६॥

तं यज्ञं बर्हिषि प्रौक्षन्पुरुषं जातमग्रतः।
तेन देवा अयजन्त साध्या ऋषयश्च ये॥ ७॥

तस्माद्यज्ञात्सर्वहुतः सम्भृतं पृषदाज्यम्।
पशून्ताञ्श्चक्रे वायव्यानारण्यान् ग्राम्याश्च ये॥ ८॥

तस्माद्यज्ञात्सर्वहुत ऋचः सामानि जज्ञिरे।
छन्दांसि जज्ञिरे तस्माद्यजुस्तस्मादजायत॥ ९॥

तस्मादश्वा अजायन्त ये के चोभयादतः।
गावो ह जज्ञिरे तस्मात्तस्माज्जाता अजावयः॥ १०॥

यत्पुरुषं व्यदधुः कतिधा व्यकल्पयन्।
मुखं किमस्य कौ बाहू का ऊरू पादा उच्येते॥ ११॥

ब्राह्मणोऽस्य मुखमासीद् बाहू राजन्यः कृतः।
ऊरू तदस्य यद्वैश्यः पद्भ्यां शूद्रो अजायत॥ १२॥

चन्द्रमा मनसो जातश्चक्षोः सूर्यो अजायत।
मुखादिन्द्रश्चाग्निश्च प्राणाद्वायुरजायत॥ १३॥

नाभ्या आसीदन्तरिक्षं शीर्ष्णो द्यौः समवर्तत।
पद्भ्यां भूमिर्दिशः श्रोत्रात्तथा लोकान् अकल्पयन्॥ १४॥

सप्तास्यासन् परिधयस्त्रिः सप्त समिधः कृताः।
देवा यद्यज्ञं तन्वाना अबध्नन्पुरुषं पशुम्॥ १५॥

यज्ञेन यज्ञमयजन्त देवास्तानि धर्माणि प्रथमान्यासन्।
ते ह नाकं महिमानः सचन्त यत्र पूर्वे साध्याः सन्ति देवाः॥ १६॥

इति ऋग्वेदीयं पुरुषसूक्तं सम्पूर्णम्॥

## Transliteration

Sahasrashirsha Purushah Sahasrakshah Sahasrapat, Sa Bhumim Vishvato Vritvatyatishthaddashangulam. (1)

Purusha Evedam Sarvam Yadbhutam Yachcha Bhavyam, Utamritatvasyeshano Yadannenatirohati. (2)

Etavanasya Mahimato Jyayamshcha Purushah, Padoasya Vishva Bhutani Tripadasyamritam Divi. (3)

Tripadurdhva Udaitpurushah Padoasyehabhavatpunah, Tato Vishvang Vyakramatsashananashane Abhi. (4)

Tasmadviradajayata Virajo Adhi Purushah, Sa Jato Atyarichyata Pashchadbhumimatho Purah. (5)

Yatpurushena Havisha Deva Yajnyamatanvata, Vasanto Asyasidajyam Grishma Idhmah Sharaddhavih. (6)

Tam Yajnyam Barhishi Praukshanpurusham Jatamagratah, Tena Deva Ayajanta Sadhya Rishayashcha Ye. (7)

Tasmadyajnyatsarvahutah Sambhritam Prishadajyam, Pashuntanshchakre Vayavyanaranyan Gramyashcha Ye. (8)

Tasmadyajnyatsarvahuta Richah Samani Jajnyire, Chhandamsi Jajnyire Tasmadyajustasmadajayata. (9)

Tasmadashva Ajayanta Ye Ke Chobhayadatah, Gavo Ha Jajnyire Tasmattasmajjata Ajavayah. (10)

Yatpurusham Vyadadhuh Katidha Vyakalpayan, Mukham Kimasya Kau Bahu Ka Uru Pada Uchyete. (11)

Brahmanoasya Mukhamasid Bahu Rajanyah Kritah, Uru Tadasya Yadvaishyah Padbhyam Shudro Ajayata. (12)

Chandrama Manaso Jatashchakshoh Suryo Ajayata, Mukhadindrashchagnishcha Pranadvayurajayata. (13)

Nabhya Asidantariksham Shirshno Dyauh Samavartata, Padbhyam Bhumirdishah Shrotrattatha Lokan Akalpayan. (14)

Saptasyasan Paridhayastrih Sapta Samidhah Kritah, Deva Yadyajnyam Tanvana Abadhnanpurusham Pashum. (15)

Yajnyena Yajnyamayajanta Devastani Dharmani Prathamanyasan, Te Ha Nakam Mahimanah Sachanta Yatra Purve Sadhyah Santi Devah. (16)

Iti Rigvediyam Purushasuktam Sampurnam.

## Meaning

The complete Purusha Sukta (Rigveda 10.90), one of the most ancient and important Vedic hymns, describing the cosmic sacrifice (yajna) of the primordial Cosmic Being (Purusha). It declares Purusha as having a thousand heads, eyes and feet, pervading the universe and extending beyond it; he is identified with everything that has existed and will exist, with only a quarter of him manifest as the visible world while three-quarters remain in the immortal transcendent. The hymn describes how, in the first cosmic sacrifice, the gods offered Purusha himself as the oblation, and from this sacrifice the seasons, the various classes of beings, the Vedas, horses and cattle, and finally the four social orders (Brahmins from his mouth, Kshatriyas from his arms, Vaishyas from his thighs, and Shudras from his feet) all came into being. It concludes by describing how the Moon, Sun, Indra, Agni and Vayu arose from his mind, eyes, mouth and breath, and how the sky, earth, and directions arose from his head, feet and ears — establishing the entire cosmos as a manifestation of this single, primordial sacrifice.

## Benefits

One of the most ancient and widely recited hymns of the Rigveda, chanted during Vishnu and Narayana worship, temple consecrations (kumbhabhishekam), fire rituals (homas) and major life ceremonies. It is considered to confer cosmic consciousness, a sense of the underlying unity of all creation, and is believed to invoke the protection and blessings of the Supreme Being in all major Vedic rites. It is commonly recited alongside the Sri Suktam and other Vedic suktas during elaborate puja and yagna ceremonies.
