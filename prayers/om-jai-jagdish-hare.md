## Sanskrit

ॐ जय जगदीश हरे, स्वामी जय जगदीश हरे। भक्त जनों के संकट, क्षण में दूर करे॥ जो ध्यावे फल पावे, दुख बिनसे मन का। सुख सम्पत्ति घर आवे, कष्ट मिटे तन का॥ मात पिता तुम मेरे, शरण गहूँ मैं किसकी। तुम बिन और न दूजा, आस करूँ जिसकी॥ तुम पूरण परमात्मा, तुम अन्तर्यामी। पारब्रह्म परमेश्वर, तुम सबके स्वामी॥ तुम करुणा के सागर, तुम पालनकर्ता। मैं मूरख खल कामी, कृपा करो भर्ता॥ तुम हो एक अगोचर, सबके प्राणपति। किस विध मिलूँ दयामय, तुमको मैं कुमति॥ दीनबंधु दुखहर्ता, तुम रक्षक मेरे। करुणा हस्त बढ़ाओ, द्वार पड़ा तेरे॥ विषय विकार मिटाओ, पाप हरो देवा। श्रद्धा भक्ति बढ़ाओ, संतन की सेवा॥ तन मन धन सब तेरा, सब कुछ है तेरा। तेरा तुझको अर्पण, क्या लागे मेरा॥

## Transliteration

## Meaning

Victory to you, O Lord of the Universe! You instantly remove the difficulties of your devotees. Whoever meditates upon you receives the fruit — sorrow of the mind vanishes, happiness and prosperity come home and bodily suffering ends. You are my mother and father — to whose shelter shall I go? Without you there is no other upon whom I can place my hope. You are the perfect Supreme Soul, the all-knowing inner witness, the Supreme Brahman and Lord of all. You are the ocean of compassion and the sustainer of all — I am foolish, wicked and full of desire; show me your grace, O Lord. You are the one unseen Lord, the master of all beings — how shall I, with my poor understanding, find you, O compassionate one? O friend of the downtrodden and remover of sorrow, you are my protector — extend your merciful hand, for I stand at your door. Remove the worldly vices and sins, O Lord — increase my faith, devotion and service to the saints. Body, mind and wealth are all yours, everything belongs to you — what offering is truly mine when I return to you only what is already yours.

## Benefits

The most universally sung Hindu aarti, composed by Pt. Shradha Ram Phillauri in 1870 and offered to Lord Vishnu as the Lord of the Universe. Performed in homes and temples at dusk across India, it moves from praise and petition to complete surrender — concluding with the profound offering that body, mind and all possessions belong to God alone. It is believed to remove suffering, bestow happiness and prosperity and deepen the devotees faith and devotion.
