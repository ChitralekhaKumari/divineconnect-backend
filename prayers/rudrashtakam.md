## Sanskrit

नमामीशमीशान निर्वाणरूपं विभुं व्यापकं ब्रह्मवेदस्वरूपम्।
निजं निर्गुणं निर्विकल्पं निरीहं चिदाकाशमाकाशवासं भजेऽहम्॥ १॥

निराकारमोङ्कारमूलं तुरीयं गिराज्ञानगोतीतमीशं गिरीशम्।
करालं महाकालकालं कृपालं गुणागारसंसारपारं नतोऽहम्॥ २॥

तुषाराद्रिसंकाशगौरं गभीरं मनोभूतकोटिप्रभाश्री शरीरम्।
स्फुरन्मौलिकल्लोलिनी चारुगङ्गा लसद्भालबालेन्दु कण्ठे भुजङ्गा॥ ३॥

चलत्कुण्डलं भ्रू सुनेत्रं विशालं प्रसन्नाननं नीलकण्ठं दयालम्।
मृगाधीशचर्माम्बरं मुण्डमालं प्रियं शंकरं सर्वनाथं भजामि॥ ४॥

प्रचण्डं प्रकृष्टं प्रगल्भं परेशं अखण्डं अजं भानुकोटिप्रकाशम्।
त्रयः शूलनिर्मूलनं शूलपाणिं भजेऽहं भवानीपतिं भावगम्यम्॥ ५॥

कलातीतकल्याणकल्पान्तकारी सदा सज्जनानन्ददाता पुरारी।
चिदानन्दसन्दोह मोहापहारी प्रसीद प्रसीद प्रभो मन्मथारी॥ ६॥

न यावदुमानाथपादारविन्दं भजन्तीह लोके परे वा नराणाम्।
न तावत्सुखं शान्ति सन्तापनाशं प्रसीद प्रभो सर्वभूतादिवासम्॥ ७॥

न जानामि योगं जपं नैव पूजां नतोऽहं सदा सर्वदा शम्भु तुभ्यम्।
जराजन्मदुःखैरतोऽहं दीनं सिदाक्षिमां पाहि मामीश शम्भो॥ ८॥

रुद्राष्टकमिदं प्रोक्तं विप्रेण हरतोषये।
ये पठन्ति नरा भक्त्या तेषां शम्भुः प्रसीदति॥ ९॥

इति श्रीगोस्वामितुलसीदासकृतं श्रीरुद्राष्टकं सम्पूर्णम्॥

## Transliteration

Namamishamishana Nirvanarupam Vibhum Vyapakam Brahmavedasvarupam, Nijam Nirgunam Nirvikalpam Niriham Chidakashamakashavasam Bhajeaham. (1)

Nirakaramomkaramulam Turiyam Giragyanagotitamisham Girisham, Karalam Mahakalakalam Kripalam Gunagarasamsaraparam Natoaham. (2)

Tusharadrisamkashagauram Gabhiram Manobhutakotiprabhashri Shariram, Sphuranmaulikallolini Charuganga Lasadbhalabalendu Kanthe Bhujanga. (3)

Chalatkundalam Bhru Sunetram Vishalam Prasannananam Nilakantham Dayalam, Mrigadhishacharmambaram Mundamalam Priyam Shankaram Sarvanatham Bhajami. (4)

Prachandam Prakrishtam Pragalbham Paresham Akhandam Ajam Bhanukotiprakasham, Trayah Shulanirmulanam Shulapanim Bhajeaham Bhavanipatim Bhavagamyam. (5)

Kalatitakalyanakalpantakari Sada Sajjananandadata Purari, Chidanandasandoha Mohapahari Prasida Prasida Prabho Manmathari. (6)

Na Yavadumanathapadaravindam Bhajantiha Loke Pare Va Naranam, Na Tavatsukham Shanti Santapanasham Prasida Prabho Sarvabhutadivasam. (7)

Na Janami Yogam Japam Naiva Pujam Natoaham Sada Sarvada Shambhu Tubhyam, Jarajanmaduhkhairatoaham Dinam Sidakshimam Pahi Mamisha Shambho. (8)

Rudrashtakamidam Proktam Viprena Haratoshaye, Ye Pathanti Nara Bhaktya Tesham Shambhuh Prasidati. (9)

Iti Shri Goswami Tulasidasakritam Shri Rudrashtakam Sampurnam.

## Meaning

An eight-verse hymn composed by Goswami Tulsidas (author of the Ramcharitmanas) glorifying Lord Shiva in his Rudra form, appearing in the Uttara Kanda of the Ramcharitmanas. It opens by bowing to Shiva as the formless embodiment of liberation, pervading all and identical with Brahman and the Vedas (1); describes him as the formless root of Om, beyond speech and knowledge, fearsome yet compassionate, the death of death itself (2); paints his iconic physical form — snow-white and profound, the Ganga flowing from his crown, the crescent moon and serpent at his throat (3); his swinging earrings, broad eyes, blue throat, deerskin garment and garland of skulls (4); his fierce, complete, eternal and unborn nature, shining like a million suns, holding the trident, the consort of Bhavani (5); his role as the giver of bliss to the virtuous, dispeller of illusion, destroyer of Kamadeva (6); declares there is no peace without devotion to his lotus feet (7); and closes with Tulsidas's personal surrender — admitting he knows no yoga, chanting or ritual worship, but simply and always bows to Shambhu, asking to be protected from the sorrows of old age and birth (8). The ninth verse states the hymn's benefit.

## Benefits

Recited daily in Shiva worship, especially during Shravan month, on Mondays, during the evening Sandhya Aarti, and on Maha Shivaratri. The text's own closing verse promises that Lord Shambhu (Shiva) becomes ever pleased with anyone who recites this hymn with devotion. Its final verse of complete surrender — admitting ignorance of yoga, chanting and ritual while bowing wholeheartedly to Shiva — is considered one of the most powerful expressions of devotional surrender in Hindu literature, and is believed to remove the sorrows of birth, old age and the cycle of worldly suffering through Shiva's grace.
