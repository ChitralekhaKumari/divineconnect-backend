## Sanskrit

श्री गुरु चरन सरोज रज, निज मन मुकुर सुधारि।
बरनऊं रघुबर बिमल जसु, जो दायक फल चारि॥
बुद्धिहीन तनु जानिके, सुमिरौं पवन-कुमार।
बल बुद्धि विद्या देहु मोहि, हरहु कलेस विकार॥
चौपाई:
जय हनुमान ज्ञान गुन सागर। जय कपीस तिहुं लोक उजागर॥ 1
राम दूत अतुलित बल धामा। अंजनि-पुत्र पवनसुत नामा॥ 2
महावीर विक्रम बजरंगी। कुमति निवार सुमति के संगी॥ 3
कंचन बरन बिराज सुबेसा। कानन कुण्डल कुंचित केसा॥ 4
हाथ बज्र औ ध्वजा बिराजे। कांधे मूंज जनेऊ साजे॥ 5
शंकर सुवन केसरी नंदन। तेज प्रताप महा जग बंदन॥ 6
विद्यावान गुनी अति चातुर। राम काज करिबे को आतुर॥ 7
प्रभु चरित्र सुनिबे को रसिया। राम लखन सीता मन बसिया॥ 8
सूक्ष्म रूप धरि सियहिं दिखावा। बिकट रूप धरि लंक जरावा॥ 9
भीम रूप धरि असुर संहारे। रामचंद्र के काज संवारे॥ 10
लाय सजीवन लखन जियाये। श्रीरघुबीर हरषि उर लाये॥ 11
रघुपति कीन्ही बहुत बड़ाई। तुम मम प्रिय भरतहि सम भाई॥ 12
सहस बदन तुम्हरो जस गावैं। अस कहि श्रीपति कंठ लगावैं॥ 13
सनकादिक ब्रह्मादि मुनीसा। नारद सारद सहित अहीसा॥ 14
जम कुबेर दिगपाल जहां ते। कवि कोविद कहि सके कहां ते॥ 15
तुम उपकार सुग्रीवहिं कीन्हा। राम मिलाय राज पद दीन्हा॥ 16
तुम्हरो मंत्र विभीषण माना। लंकेश्वर भए सब जग जाना॥ 17
जुग सहस्र जोजन पर भानू। लील्यो ताहि मधुर फल जानू॥ 18
प्रभु मुद्रिका मेलि मुख माहीं। जलधि लांघि गये अचरज नाहीं॥ 19
दुर्गम काज जगत के जेते। सुगम अनुग्रह तुम्हरे तेते॥ 20
राम दुआरे तुम रखवारे। होत न आज्ञा बिनु पैसारे॥ 21
सब सुख लहैं तुम्हारी सरना। तुम रक्षक काहू को डर ना॥ 22
आपन तेज सम्हारो आपे। तीनों लोक हांक ते कांपे॥ 23
भूत पिशाच निकट नहिं आवे। महावीर जब नाम सुनावे॥ 24
नासे रोग हरे सब पीरा। जपत निरंतर हनुमत बीरा॥ 25
संकट से हनुमान छुड़ावे। मन क्रम बचन ध्यान जो लावे॥ 26
सब पर राम तपस्वी राजा। तिन के काज सकल तुम साजा॥ 27
और मनोरथ जो कोई लावे। सोई अमित जीवन फल पावे॥ 28
चारों जुग परताप तुम्हारा। है परसिद्ध जगत उजियारा॥ 29
साधु-संत के तुम रखवारे। असुर निकंदन राम दुलारे॥ 30
अष्ट सिद्धि नव निधि के दाता। अस बर दीन जानकी माता॥ 31
राम रसायन तुम्हरे पासा। सदा रहो रघुपति के दासा॥ 32
तुम्हरे भजन राम को पावे। जनम-जनम के दुख बिसरावे॥ 33
अंत काल रघुबर पुर जाई। जहां जन्म हरि-भक्त कहाई॥ 34
और देवता चित्त न धरई। हनुमत सेई सर्व सुख करई॥ 35
संकट कटे मिटे सब पीरा। जो सुमिरै हनुमत बलबीरा॥ 36
जय जय जय हनुमान गोसाईं। कृपा करहु गुरुदेव की नाईं॥ 37
जो सत बार पाठ कर कोई। छूटहि बंदि महा सुख होई॥ 38
जो यह पढ़े हनुमान चालीसा। होय सिद्धि साखी गौरीसा॥ 39
तुलसीदास सदा हरि चेरा। कीजे नाथ हृदय मंह डेरा॥ 40
दोहा:
पवन तनय संकट हरन, मंगल मूरति रूप।
राम लखन सीता सहित, हृदय बसहु सुर भूप॥

## Meaning

Opening Dohas: With the dust of my Guru's lotus feet, I first cleanse the mirror of my mind, then narrate the pure glory of Sri Raghubar (Rama), which grants the four fruits of life. Knowing myself devoid of wisdom, I remember the son of the Wind, Hanuman - grant me strength, wisdom and knowledge, and remove my afflictions.
1: Victory to Hanuman, ocean of wisdom and virtue; victory to the King of monkeys, illuminator of the three worlds.
2: You are Rama's messenger, an abode of matchless strength; son of Anjani, known as the son of the Wind.
3: Great hero, mighty and valiant, dispeller of evil thoughts and companion of good sense.
4: Golden-hued and well adorned, wearing earrings, with curly hair.
5: You hold a mace and a flag, with a sacred thread across your shoulder.
6: Son of Shiva and Kesari, your glory and radiance are worshipped throughout the world.
7: Learned, virtuous and extremely clever, ever eager to accomplish Rama's tasks.
8: You delight in hearing of the Lord's deeds; Rama, Lakshmana and Sita dwell in your heart.
9: You showed Sita a tiny, subtle form, then took a massive form to burn Lanka.
10: You took a terrifying form to destroy demons, accomplishing Rama's mission.
11: You brought the Sanjeevani herb to revive Lakshmana; Rama embraced you with joy.
12: Raghupati (Rama) praised you greatly, calling you as dear as his own brother Bharat.
13: A thousand mouths sing your glory, said the Lord as he embraced you close.
14: Sanaka and other sages, Brahma, Narada, Sharada and the serpent king all praise you.
15: Even Yama, Kubera, the guardians of the directions and learned poets cannot fully describe your glory.
16: You helped Sugriva, uniting him with Rama and restoring his kingdom.
17: Vibhishana accepted your counsel, and thus became King of Lanka, known to all the world.
18: Thinking the sun a sweet fruit, you swallowed it, though it lies thousands of yojanas away.
19: Holding Rama's ring in your mouth, you crossed the ocean with ease - no wonder at all.
20: All difficult tasks of the world become easy through your grace.
21: You stand guard at Rama's door; none may enter without your permission.
22: All who take refuge in you attain happiness; under your protection there is no fear.
23: You alone can withstand your own power; the three worlds tremble at your roar.
24: Ghosts and evil spirits cannot come near one who chants the name of the great hero Hanuman.
25: All diseases and pains vanish for one who constantly repeats the name of the brave Hanuman.
26: Hanuman removes troubles for those who remember him in thought, word and deed.
27: Rama, the great ascetic king, entrusted all his works to you.
28: Whoever comes to you with any desire obtains the abundant fruit of life.
29: Your glory pervades all four ages, renowned and radiant throughout the world.
30: You are the protector of saints and devotees, beloved of Rama, destroyer of demons.
31: You grant the eight siddhis and nine treasures, a boon given to you by Mother Sita.
32: You always possess the elixir of devotion to Rama, and remain forever His servant.
33: Through devotion to you, one attains Rama and forgets the sorrows of countless lifetimes.
34: At the end of life, one goes to Rama's divine abode, forever known as a devotee of Hari.
35: One who serves no other deity but worships Hanuman attains complete happiness.
36: All troubles and pains disappear for one who remembers the mighty, brave Hanuman.
37: Victory, victory, victory to Hanuman! Bestow your grace on me as a true Guru would.
38: Whoever recites this a hundred times is freed from bondage and attains great joy.
39: Whoever reads this Hanuman Chalisa attains success, with Lord Shiva himself as witness.
40: Tulsidas, ever a servant of Hari, prays: O Lord, please dwell forever in my heart.
Closing Doha: O son of the Wind, remover of troubles, embodiment of auspiciousness - dwell in my heart along with Rama, Lakshmana and Sita, O king of the gods.

## Benefits

Opening Dohas: Reciting these invokes the Guru's blessings, purifies the mind, and grants strength, wisdom and freedom from affliction before beginning the Chalisa.
1: Invokes wisdom and dispels ignorance, illuminating the mind just as Hanuman lights up the three worlds.
2: Builds courage and unwavering strength through Hanuman's role as Rama's devoted messenger.
3: Clears confused or negative thoughts, replacing them with clarity and good sense.
4: Brings radiance, positive energy and confidence to one's presence.
5: Instills discipline, steadfastness and readiness to serve one's duty.
6: Grants protection and inner strength drawn from Hanuman's divine lineage.
7: Sharpens intellect and aids learning, especially valued by students and seekers of knowledge.
8: Deepens devotion and keeps the heart focused on Rama, Lakshmana and Sita.
9: Grants adaptability and courage to face any situation, however small or overwhelming.
10: Bestows the strength needed to overcome powerful obstacles and negative forces.
11: Invoked for healing and swift recovery from illness, recalling Hanuman reviving Lakshmana.
12: Strengthens bonds of loyalty, trust and brotherhood between devotees.
13: Brings recognition, respect and lasting good name.
14: Connects the devotee to the blessings of sages and divine wisdom.
15: Cultivates humility, reminding even the learned of Hanuman's boundless glory.
16: Helps restore what was lost - opportunity, position or standing - as with Sugriva's kingdom.
17: Grants sound guidance and wise counsel in difficult decisions.
18: Builds confidence to attempt tasks that seem impossible.
19: Eases journeys and travel, removing obstacles along the way.
20: Turns the most difficult tasks easy through Hanuman's grace.
21: Offers protection and security, like a guardian standing watch at the door.
22: Removes fear for those who take refuge in Hanuman's care.
23: Strengthens self-control and command over one's own energy and impulses.
24: Protects against ghosts, evil spirits and negative energies when Hanuman's name is invoked.
25: Removes disease and physical suffering for those who chant his name without fail.
26: Relieves troubles and hardships for devotees who remember Hanuman in thought, word and deed.
27: Blesses one's efforts and work when carried out under righteous guidance.
28: Fulfils sincere desires and wishes brought to Hanuman with devotion.
29: Bestows enduring fame and a radiant reputation across time.
30: Protects spiritual seekers and destroys negativity around them.
31: Invokes blessings of the eight siddhis (spiritual powers) and nine nidhis (forms of prosperity).
32: Deepens lifelong devotion and an unbroken connection to Rama's grace.
33: Frees the mind from sorrows and burdens carried across lifetimes through steady devotion.
34: Grants a peaceful passing and union with the divine at the end of life.
35: Brings complete happiness through single-pointed devotion to Hanuman.
36: Dissolves accumulated troubles and pain for those who call upon Hanuman's strength.
37: Invokes divine grace and guidance, much like that of a true Guru.
38: Reciting the Chalisa 100 times is said to free one from bondage and bestow great happiness - a practice often used for overcoming major life obstacles.
39: Regular recitation is believed to grant siddhi (spiritual accomplishment) and success in one's endeavours.
40: Deepens a lifetime of devotion and service, following in Tulsidas's own example.
Closing Doha: Invites Hanuman to remove all troubles and invokes the combined presence and protection of Rama, Lakshmana and Sita in the devotee's heart.
